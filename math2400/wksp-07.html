Problem 2

Let $f \colon [0,1] \to \R$ be defined by $f(x) := x$. Show that $f$ is Riemann integrable, i.e. $f \in \mathcal R[0,1]$, and compute $\int_0^1 f$ using the definition of the integral.








Problem 3

Suppose there exists a sequence of partitions $P_k$ of $[a,b]$ such that $\lim_{k\ra \infty} \bigg(U(P_k, f) - L(P_k, f)\bigg) = 0.$ Show that $f$ is Riemann integrable and that $\int_a^b f = \lim_{k\ra \infty} U(P_k, f) =\lim_{k\ra \infty} L(P_k, f).$






Problem 5

(Mean Value Theorem for integrals) Prove that if $f:[a,b]\ra \R$ is continuous, then there exists a $c\in [a,b]$ such that $\int_a^bf=f(c) (b-a)$.
